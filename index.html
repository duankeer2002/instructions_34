General Instructions

Imagine that you own a house for two periods and plan to sell it at the end of period 2. The house is worth 100,000 talers at the beginning of period 1. A “taler” is a hypothetical currency unit, similar to U.S. dollars or British pounds.

At the beginning of period 1, your total assets consist of the value of the house plus 5,000 talers in cash.

A strong hurricane may occur in either period of the two-period game. Note that the hurricane may occur in period 1 or period 2, in both periods, or in neither period.

If a hurricane occurs in a given period, your house will suffer a loss of 50,000 talers. If you purchase insurance, you will be protected against this loss.

If you purchase insurance and a hurricane occurs, the insurance company will compensate you for the loss so that you can rebuild the house to its pre-hurricane value.

If you do not purchase insurance and a hurricane occurs, the value of your house will decrease by 50,000 talers.

Therefore, at the end of each period, the value of your house depends on whether a hurricane occurred and whether you purchased insurance.

Your total assets at the end of period 2 equal: “The value of your house at the end of period 2” + “5,000 talers in cash” – "All insurance premiums paid in both periods combined"

The probability of a hurricane in period 1 is 1/25 (4%). If no hurricane occurs in period 1, the probability in period 2 remains 1/25. However, if a hurricane occurs in period 1, the probability in period 2 increases to 1/20 (5%). Whether a hurricane occurs is determined randomly by the computer.

You may choose to purchase a one-period insurance contract or a two-period insurance contract to protect your house from potential hurricane losses.

One-Period Insurance Contract

Period 1: 2,000 talers

Period 2 (no hurricane in period 1): 2,000 talers

Period 2 (hurricane in period 1): 2,500 talers

OR

Two-Period Insurance Contract (you purchase insurance for both periods at the beginning of period 1)

Period 1: 2,010 talers

Period 2: 2,010 talers

Game Rules

After your period 1 decision, the computer displays whether a hurricane occurred.

If you purchased the two-period insurance contract, you are automatically covered in period 2.

If you did not purchase the two-period contract or only purchased one-period insurance in period 1, you must decide again at the beginning of period 2 whether to purchase insurance.

After completing the first two-period game, you will begin a new two-period game under the same rules. You will complete 30 such two-period games.
